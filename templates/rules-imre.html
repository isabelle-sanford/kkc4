Imre

Every term/full cycle, players (students and Masters alike) can go to Imre once (except for those living in The Grey Man or The Pearl of Imre). For the month they are in Imre, students cannot impress (file EP with) a Master and Masters cannot elevate students, though students can still be elevated. Players cannot do any University action (use a field ability or create an item), but they can do anything in Imre. They may still send PMs to anyone at the University (assuming they’re not expelled).

Imre has a whole host of possibilities that aren’t necessarily available at the University. There are 5 locations players can stop by:

  • The Eolian
  • Nox's Apothecary
  • The Moneylenders: Devi or Giles
  • The Loaded Dice
  • The Black Market

Items for sale at the Apothecary and Black Market will be given to each player going to Imre at the beginning of the cycle they are heading there. Players can also sell items and contracts on the Black Market. Contracts are sold for whatever the seller thinks they are worth.

Players can go to the Eolian once per month, though they can only try for their Talent Pipes once. Players can go to The Loaded Dice once per month, unless they are blacklisted by the establishment. Players can only go to Devi’s or Giles’ once per term. They can purchase/sell any number of items/contracts at the Black Market or the Apothecary, while supplies last.

The Eolian

  • Each player can try for their Talent Pipes once in the game. An attempt costs 1 talent, nonrefundable. Every player has a randomly-distributed base stat for their character’s musical ability, and when you start the game, you will know the average musical talent of your character. As stated in the Application System section above, players can choose to submit a recording showcasing their musical ability in order to boost this stat pre-game.
    • All players are able boost their stats in-game by going to the Eolian and practicing. Each practice will boost your base stat by .5. You may only practice once per time in Imre.
  • When you try for the Pipes, the GM will roll a die for your performance, and that will be added to your base stat. This means that a really good musician who has a bad performance might not get the Pipes, while a mediocre musician who has a really good performance could. You’ll never know unless you try.
  • Talent Pipes give an additional 10 talents per cycle to any player who holds them. Talent Pipes can be given to other players, but they cannot be stolen. If given away, the player who initially won them cannot win another set.

Nox's Apothecary

Each month, a variety of items/services are available for purchase by anyone who wants to pay the cost. The courier service is always available, but the items are based on inventory and may run out. Should an item run out, players will have to wait for it to be restocked. Restocking happens at the beginning of each term. You may also fail to purchase it if it runs out while you and many others are trying to buy it.

Important Note: Most Apothecary items can be destroyed.

  • Nahlrout: 1 talent per ounce. Protects against lashing effects when used on self; action-cancellation when used on someone else. (one-time use per ounce)
  • Couriers: 2 talents per message. Sends an anonymous message to any player, delivered at the end of the month.
  • Bloodless: 10 talents. Protects against sabotage.
  • Gram: 25 talents. Stops kill/sabotage/malignant sympathy actions against the holder. (cannot be destroyed) May only be used twice.

Moneylenders

There are two moneylenders that are available to go to in Imre. When borrowing money from the moneylenders, the amount of interest is due at the end of each term. Players who default on a debt may not take out another.

  • Giles'

    Only players who are staying at The Grey Man (or Cealdish Commoners) can go to Giles’. Giles will give loans up to 2 talents and has an interest rate of 15% per term. Players are not able to put items up for collateral to increase the loan amount like they can with Devi. If you default on a loan with Giles, he will let the owners of The Grey Man know, and you will be blacklisted from their establishment.

  • Devi's

    Each player can go to Devi’s to borrow money from her. The minimum loaned is 4 talents and the maximum is half of the player’s term income. (Players can also put up items for collateral to increase the loan amount.) Interest starts accruing immediately, at a rate of 30% per term, so if a student borrows 4 talents, the interest will be 1 talent and 2 jots, making the full amount owed 5 talents and 2 jots.

    If students cannot pay the interest, Devi will make a mommet with the player’s blood, and that mommet will go into the Black Market for other players to purchase, with a cost equal to the player’s debt.

The Loaded Dice

Everyone likes being risky every once in a while, right? The Loaded Dice fulfills all your gambling needs. Don’t let the name throw you off. We’re totally honest here!

Every player can come to The Loaded Dice and play a game. The house rolls 1d20 and the gambler is able to bet on up to 5 numbers. The more numbers you bet on, the higher your chances of winning but the lower your winnings will be. You can bet a maximum of 1 talent and a minimum of 1 drab and you will win the ratio of the denomination you bet. If your number comes up, you win! If it doesn’t, you lose! Straight-forward, right? If you win, you cannot return to The Loaded Dice for a full term (1 cycle). If you win twice in 2 terms / cycles, the owners of The Loaded Dice will blacklist you for 4 terms / cycles. After all, we’re honest. You better be too.

Winnings Ratio
  • 1 bet = 20:1
  • 2 bet = 15:1
  • 3 bet = 10:1
  • 4 bet = 5:1
  • 5 bet = 2:1

The Black Market

The Black Market can be separated into 2 distinct sections: For Sale and Contracts.

For Sale

This section of the Black Market is populated partially by the GM with certain items available based on inventory. These items can run out, but will be restocked every few cycles. Services provided in the Black Market are always available to players. Players can also anonymously sell items/services on this part of the Black Market. These items/services are sold for the amount chosen by the player, so they can be as cheap or expensive as you want.

  • Mommets: Price is the player’s debt to Devi. Mommets can be used for role-blocking, effectively cancelling all of that player’s actions (complaint, impressing, etc) the turn it is used (use does take up your own action). If multiple people try to buy the same mommet while in Imre, only the first will succeed.
  • Bodyguards: Variable. When purchasing the bodyguard, you tell them who they are to protect, and that’s who they will protect for as long as they are able. The bodyguard cannot be transferred to another player. Protects from kills, sabotages, and theft. Will protect from theft indefinitely, but can only protect from 2 sabotages, and 1 kill. (Note: if the bodyguard protects from a sabotage one turn and a kill comes the next, he will protect from both, but if both come through on the same turn, he will only protect from the kill. If theft comes through in either of those scenarios as well, he will protect from the theft in all cases). Bodyguards are not transferable between players.
  • Assassins: Variable. Kills a player of your choice, even if they are still attending the University. Can be blocked by actions/items that stop a kill, but cannot be blocked by actions/items that only stop sabotages.
Contracts

The Contracts half of the Black Market is populated solely by players. Players sell contracts anonymously, with the price of the contract paid to the person who fulfills the contract. The amount tied to the contract is held in escrow by the GMs until the contract is completed, and then it’s paid upon delivery. There are no limits to the types of contracts that can be sold. For some ideas of the types of things that can be sold: kill a particular person, intercept a person’s messages, bribe a Master’s vote or a player’s complaint, provide information, etc.

If a player accepts a contract, they will not be told who sold it, but they must fulfill the contract by the end of the next cycle. If they do not, the contract returns to the Black Market, and not only does the one who previously accepted it not get anything, but they also cannot go back into the Black Market and accept it again.

Example 1: Elbereth goes to Imre and sells a contract for someone to sabotage Lumgol for 100 talents. Stink goes to Imre and only sees “Sabotage Lumgol - 100 talents”. The seller of the contract remains anonymous. Should Stink choose to accept this contract, he must sabotage Lumgol the next cycle. Once he has, El loses 100 talents and Stink gains 100, but El won’t know who took the contract and Stink still won’t know who sold it.
Example 2: Lumgol goes to Imre and sees a contract. “Sabotage Lumgol - 100 talents.” Shocked and horrified at the level of betrayal, she accepts this contract, solely to keep others from profiting from her demise. At the end of the next cycle, she has not sabotaged herself, so the contract returns to the Black Market. Lumgol returns to Imre that same turn, but in the list of contracts available she receives, that contract is not included, even though it is in the Black Market.
Note: Contracts cannot have constraints limiting them to only 1 person or class of people (e.g. “I want an Edema Ruh to...”).
