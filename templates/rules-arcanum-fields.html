Arcanum Fields

There are 9 different fields of study for students at the University. By impressing a Master (filing EP) and being elevated into a field, students learn abilities in that field. Most fields have 4 role abilities accessible. By impressing a Master multiple times and being elevated multiple times, students increase their knowledge in that field, and gain another ability.

While most fields have role abilities, Alchemy and Artificery students are able to create items. Items can be kept by the student who made it, or they can be given away to other players. The Masters of Alchemy and Artificery do not give their students items to create. Each item in those fields requires students to be a certain level before they can make that item. Items take 2 action periods to create. This can be spread through one turn or two. If a student uses two action periods in a single turn to make an item quickly, they will not be able to file a complaint or impress a Master (flavor-wise, because they’re too busy creating the item to focus on anything else).

Linguistics

The Linguistics field works solely with words and languages. The abilities are basic, but they get more powerful when combined together. Additionally, students who stick with Linguistics gain a little extra.

  • Second level upgrade: Gain a second action period.
  • Third level upgrade: Gain a third action period.
  • Master level upgrade: Gain a fourth action period.

These are not Linguistic specific periods; they are effectively extra general action periods.

  • Hand Delivery
    E'lir

    You’re paranoid and hand-deliver your messages to the recipients.

    No one can peek at your messages, and you may send them to anyone (even if they are expelled).

    This is a passive ability that cannot be roleblocked.
  • Mysterious Bulletins
    Re'lar

    In the middle of the night, you and a few friends post a message on as many surfaces around the University as you can.

    You can send an anonymous message in the writeup. The message can be as long as you like and it can contain words and images.

  • Bribe the Messenger
    El'the

    You’ve made friends with the messengers and for a few drabs, they’ll let you take a peek at some of the messages your fellow students send.

    You can spy on the messages of 1 player. You will learn the contents of all incoming and outgoing PMs as well as the recipient or sender of the messages from that turn.

  • Linguistic Analysis
    Master

    Your analysis skills are legendary. All you need to do is read a message from someone and you just know if they’re lying or telling the truth.

    You can ask the GM if your target lied the previous turn, and will receive yes/no for an answer. You are not told how many lies were told or what they were. This only applies to communication you have access to (the thread, PMs, or potentially PMs you’ve spied on).

    If the target tells a lie which they believe to be the truth, it will return as true.

    • Arithmetics

    Master Arithmetists gain an action period. This action period is not specific to Arithmetic. All Arithmetists have access to all of their abilities.

  • Reduced Interest

    You have a knack for convincing moneylenders to give you reasonable interest rates.

    If you go to Devi or Giles, your interest is cut by 20%.

    • Second level upgrade: 30% reduction
    • Third level upgrade: 40% reduction
    • Master level upgrade: 50% reduction
    This is a passive ability and cannot be roleblocked, but going to Devi’s can be.
  • Pickpocket

    You’ve been practicing your sleight of hand while studying. You’ve gotten pretty adept at pickpocketing people and stealing some of their coins. However, since it’s pickpocketing, you can only really do it to the people who get close enough to you.

    • Second level upgrade: You take 20% of their coins.
    • Third level upgrade: You take 30% of their coins.
    • Master level upgrade: You're able to choose your target.

    Target a random person targeting you that cycle and take 10% of their coins.

  • Great Deals

    You have an eye for a good deal. Somehow, you’re able to talk your way into a deal no matter what you’re buying or selling.

    10% increase in the payout when you fulfill a Black Market contract. 10% decrease when purchasing items from the Black Market or the Apothecary.

    • Second level upgrade: 15% increase / decrease
    • Third level upgrade: 20% increase / decrease
    • Master level upgrade: 25% increase / decrease
    This is a passive ability and cannot be roleblocked, but going to the Apothecary or Black Market can be.
    These percentages do stack, so if a Cealdish Commoner was an E'lir Arithmetist, they would get a 35% increase/decrease.
  • Decreased Tuition

    5% decrease to your tuition.

    • Second level upgrade: 10% decrease
    • Third level upgrade: 15% decrease
    • Master level upgrade: 20% decrease
    This is a passive ability and cannot be roleblocked.
    • Example: Straw is an Edema Ruh and a Re’lar with two elevations in Arithmetics. He goes to Imre and visits Devi’s to get a loan. Thanks to the second level upgrade of Reduced Interest, his normal 30% interest rate is reduced by 30%, so the interest rate on his loan is now 21%.

    Rhetoric and Logic

  • Argumentum Ad Nauseam
    E'lir

    You begin a conversation with your target, educating them on the intricacies of ancient Yllish agriculture (or something equally fascinating to you), boring them so much they forget to file a complaint.

    Cancel the vote(s) of your target.

  • Proficient in Hyberbole
    Re'lar

    You have a tendency to exaggerate, especially when talking about your opponents.

    You can cast a total of 4 complaints, either against the same student or different students. These complaints are still filed in the thread, so to cast for different people, you would make a red-vote without retracting the previous vote.

    If role-blocked, only first two complaints count
  • Persuasive Arguments
    El'the

    You're very good at persuading people to your side of things.

    Change one of your target's complaints (chosen at random) to someone else.

  • Law of Contraposition
    Master

    Redirect a targeted action of another player to another player of your choice. You must specify the action being redirected, and if the player does not take that action then the redirect has no effect. Law of Contraposition cannot be roleblocked by any other action except mommets.

    • Archives

    Scrivs spend a lot of time in the Stacks and Tomes. There’s a lot of knowledge crammed in their heads, making them an unexpected sort of formidable foe.

    • Second level upgrade: Gain an Archives action period.
    • Third level upgrade: Gain a second Archives action period.
    • Master level upgrade: Gain a third Archives action period.
  • Omen Recognition
    E'lir

    You recognize the signs of all kinds of fae.

    You can investigate any event and determine whether or not it was caused by a Skindancer.

  • School Records
    Re'lar

    You can research any student, learning what fields they’ve been elevated in and what their specialties are.

    This cannot be used when not attending the University.
  • Banned Books
    El'the

    With a bit of searching, you’ve managed to find a few dangerous books deep in the Stacks.

    If you’ve studied in another field (with the exception of Naming), you may use these books to learn another of that field’s abilities, of your choice. If you have not studied in another field, you can choose which field you’d like to study and you will learn a random ability in that field.

    Learning a new ability takes 2 action periods. This can be spread out over 2 months/turns or all in the same month/turn. If you use 2 actions periods on the same turn, you will lose all other actions, including PMs and complaints, since you’re spending all your time studying in the Stacks.

    You have a (+10%) chance of getting caught the first time you research. There is a (+10%) every time you research thereafter. If you use it for 2 action periods on the same turn, there is a (+20%) chance of getting caught. If you are caught, you will be brought up on Conduct Unbecoming and receive lashes as your punishment.

    • Master level: No chance of getting caught. They’re not banned from you, after all.
    After learning an action in a new field, you cannot use an Archives action period to use that ability. You may only use a general action period or a period specific to the learned ability’s field.
  • Fae Lore
    Master

    Your fellow students may laugh at your studies, but they’ve come in awfully handy lately. While they’re all carrying around iron to protect themselves from the Skindancers, you know holly is the way to go.

    If you target a Skindancer with an action, their actions are cancelled.

    • Sympathy

    Sympathy works with Alar, and the stronger one’s Alar is, the harder it is to beat them. Those who study Sympathy are susceptible to cracking and being sent to the Crockery. Students in Sympathy have a Sympathy action period, and the Master Sympathist gains an additional Sympathy action period. Sympathy action periods can be used to make mommets, use mommets, or use Malfeasance Protection.

  • Mommet-making

    All mommets are 1-use and cannot be redirected or roleblocked by another action. When used (except at Master level), a mommet blocks all actions of its target. Mommets retain the level at which they’re created, and take 1 action period to create.

    • First level upgrade: Make a mommet from an unidentified piece of hair you’ve found (a random player), which can then be used to incapacitate that player for a turn as an action. You will learn the identity of the player once it is made.
      • (+1) insanity bonus for use.
    • Second level upgrade: Your mommet can be made from a specific player of your choosing.
      • (+2) insanity bonus for use.
    • Third level upgrade: You may target someone who was sabotaged/killed the previous cycle to search for traces of their killer/saboteur, making a mommet of what you find (though you won’t learn the target of the mommet). This can similarly be used to roleblock them as an action.
      • (+3) insanity bonus for use.
    • Master level upgrade: You may use a third-level mommet to kill the player who is its target. You can still make second level mommets, but those cannot be used to kill your target.
      • (+4) insanity bonus for use.
  • Malfeasance Protection
    • First level upgrade: Create a binding between you and another student. All actions that happen to you also affect the person you’re bound to.
      • (+1) insanity bonus for use.
    • Second level upgrade: Create a binding between you and another student, both ways. All actions that happen to you also affect the person you’re bound to, and vice versa.
      • (+2) insanity bonus for use.
    • Third level upgrade: Create a binding between you and another student. All negative actions on either person are split between the 2, and downgraded. Downgraded effects: role-blocks→no effect, plum bobs→no effect, Argumentum ad Nauseam→no effect, kills/sabotages→role-blocks (all other actions that cycle are canceled for both players: complaints, impressing, anything in Imre, etc).
      • (+3) insanity bonus for use.
    • Master level upgrade: Create a binding between you and another student. All negative effects on you are transferred to the other student and do not hit you.
      • (+4) insanity bonus for use.

    • Physicking

    The Medica is a great place to learn all about medicine and keeping people healthy through treatment and preventive care. The Master Physicker has a Physicking action period. All Physickers can access all abilities, but each ability’s use depends on how much time a person has spent studying Physicking.

  • Medica Emergency

    Due to a medical emergency, you’re spending a lot of time in the Medica to help out.

    All actions targeting you fail the turn after you place the action. Cannot be used twice in a row.

    • First level: You cannot take any other actions, including PMing or filing a complaint.
    • Second level upgrade: You’ve learned to juggle things a little better, so you can PM, but you still can’t file complaints.
    • Third level upgrade: You can both PM and file complaints.
    • Master level upgrade: You may use your Physicker action period, but all other action periods are still unavailable.
  • Medica Detainment

    Detain a player in Medica for a turn, role-blocking all of their actions for 1 turn.

    • First level: 30% chance you’ll get caught and brought up on charges of Conduct Unbecoming, with a punishment of lashings.
    • Second level upgrade: 20% chance of getting caught.
    • Third level upgrade: 10% chance of getting caught.
    • Master level upgrade: No chance of getting caught (you’re not doing anything wrong).
  • Psychological Counselling

    You provide counselling for someone at risk of cracking, helping them cope with their problems.

    Decreases the target's chances of cracking that turn. If you attempt to self-target, you will receive an increase to your insanity rather than a reduction, as talking to yourself is not a sign of a sound mind.

    • First level: (-1) reduction to insanity.
    • Second level upgrade: (-2) reduction to insanity.
    • Third level upgrade: (-3) reduction to insanity.
    • Master level upgrade: (-4) reduction to insanity.
  • Cheating Death

    Follow a player and if they’re attacked with a sabotage or kill, your amazing skills prevent them from dying. Unfortunately, this also drains you of energy, so you can’t do it twice in a row. Cannot self target.

    • First level: 50% chance their wounds from a kill attempt are beyond your abilities and they will die. 25% chance you won’t prevent a sabotage. If you don’t succeed in fully protecting them, you’ll at least succeed in delaying their death, and they will have an extra turn before they succumb to their insanity/death.
    • Second level upgrade: 50% chance their wounds from a kill attempt are beyond your abilities and they will die. Sabotages are fully prevented. If you don’t succeed in protecting someone from death, you will at least delay their death for a turn.
    • Third level upgrade: 25% chance their wounds from a kill attempt are beyond your abilities and they will die. Sabotages are fully prevented. Deaths you can’t prevent will be delayed an extra turn.
    • Master level upgrade: Both kills and sabotages are fully prevented.

    • Alchemy

    At first level, gain an Item Creation period that can be used for crafting items in Alchemy or Artificery. All alchemy items are usable only once, unless otherwise stated. Each has a risk to it that lessens as the student is elevated higher in the field. All of these items can be given away or destroyed. Those who study Alchemy are more susceptible to cracking and being sent to the Crockery. Making items will give an insanity bonus for that turn. The upgrade level of an Alchemy item is determined by the number of levels in Alchemy the user has.

  • Tenaculum
    E'lir

    Destroy an item or cancel an action.

    • (+1) insanity bonus on creation.
    • First level: 20% chance of going awry and destroying a different item or canceling a random action of the target or yourself.
    • Second level upgrade: 10% chance of going awry.
    • Third level upgrade: 100% chance it will destroy what you want or cancel what you request.
    • Master level upgrade: You have enough for 2 uses.
  • Firestop
    Re'lar

    Protects from heat-based malfeasance and bone tar as a passive self-protect.

    • (+2) insanity bonus on creation.
    • First level: 20% chance of failing and going volatile, sending the target to Medica (total role-block for that turn and the next while they recover).
    • Second level upgrade: 10% chance of failing and going volatile, sending the target to Medica (total role-block for that turn and the next while they recover).
    • Third level upgrade: No chance of failure.
    • Master level upgrade: You’ve made enough for 2 uses.
  • Plum Bob
    El'the

    Drug a player and gather information from them. When using the plum bob, player will PM the GM, informing them of the use, and the questions they want to ask, just to make sure the questions are valid.

    Players can ask a maximum of 5 basic questions (questions like “Who are the other Skindancers” and “What abilities do you have/What fields are you studying” do not count as a single question). Once the questions have been cleared, the GM will set up a PM between the 2 players during the next turn. Target must answer all questions truthfully.

    Because the plum bob is dangerous, there is a chance the target will fly into a rage. If this happens, the student who used the plum bob will be brought on the Horns for Conduct Unbecoming, receiving lashings as punishment, and cannot use nahlrout to prevent their effect. The target once in a rage may choose to stop answering questions or to answer untruthfully. The questioner is not informed if or when this happens.

    • (+3) insanity bonus on creation.
    • First level: 30% chance the target will fly into a rage on the second question. Third and fourth questions go up each +20%, with the fifth question increasing another +30%.
    • Second level upgrade: 30% chance of flying into a rage on second question, +10% on third and fourth, +30% on fifth.
    • Third level upgrade: +10% chance on second question and third questions, +20% on fourth question, +30% on fifth.
    • Master level upgrade: +10% chance on second question, third, and fourth question, +20% on fifth.
  • Bone-tar
    El'the

    Destroy a lodging (Mews or an inn).

    • (+3) insanity bonus on creation.
    • First level: 50% chance of it going volatile, and killing half of the players staying at the location (including yourself).
    • Second level upgrade: 25% chance of going volatile.
    • Third level upgrade: 10% chance of going volatile. If it does, you will be injured and cannot make any actions the following turn, as you’re recovering in Medica.
    • Master level upgrade: You may choose if it goes volatile or not. If you choose for it to go volatile, there’s a 10% chance you’ll be injured in the fire-fog and you’ll spend the next turn recovering in Medica, unable to make any actions (including disciplining).
    • Example: Hael is the Master Alchemist. He creates Bone-tar and then passes it to Burnt, who has never studied Alchemy. Burnt uses the Bone-tar to destroy The Windy Tower. Because she has no elevations in Alchemy, the Bone-tar has the first level effects, meaning that she could potentially die in the destruction.
    Example: Wilson is an Re’lar who has only been elevated in Alchemy once. She creates Plum Bob. After the Plum Bob’s creation, Elbereth uses a Thieves Lamp on Wilson and steals the Plum Bob. Elbereth happens to be an El'the with 3 elevations in Alchemy, so when she uses the stolen Plum Bob on Wilson, she gets the third level upgrade effect.

    Artificery

    • First level: Items are usable only once. Gain an Item Creation period which can be used for crafting Alchemy or Artificery items.
    • Second level upgrade: Items have a 50% chance of being usable twice (decided at the creation, so the creator knows upon creating the item if it has one or 2 uses).
    • Third level upgrade: Items are usable twice.
    • Master level upgrade: Items are usable thrice.

    Note: Players who create an item and then are elevated to a new level do not gain the upgrade on the item they created previously. Unlike Alchemy items, Artificery items retain the upgrade of the level the player was when they created them.

  • Ward
    E'lir

    Detects a random action used on you by someone else (either offensive or defensive).

    • (+1) insanity bonus for creation
  • Bloodless
    Re'lar

    Passively protects from a sabotage.

    • (+2) insanity bonus for creation
  • Thieves Lamp
    Re'lar

    Robs a player of 30% of their money and 50% of their possessions.

    • (+2) insanity bonus for creation
  • Gram
    El'the

    Passively protects from sabotages, kills, and malignant sympathy. Grams cannot be destroyed.

    • (+3) insanity bonus for creation

    • Naming

    Naming is perhaps the most arcane of all the arcane arts. Seeing the true Name of a thing or living being, if one calls the true Name, one can control it. As such, Naming works a little differently than the other fields. There are no specific abilities, but there are Names students who wish to learn Naming can learn. Each Name has a basic function, but it’s up to the Namer’s discretion on what they want to actually do with that Name.

    When the Master Namer elevates a student the first time, the student learns the Name of the Wind, and by using Wind, they can learn more Names. Once the Namer has used Wind once, they have a 25% chance of learning a new, random Name. Upon using Wind twice, they have a 50% chance of learning a new Name. This percent increases until the Namer learns a new Name. Then the percentage resets to 0%, with (+25%) each time the new Name is used. Using the Name of the Wind after they’ve learned 2 names will not increase the chance of learning a third, and so on. While the Namer is still on their first Naming rank, they can only use 1 Name per turn, regardless of how many Names they learn.

    After a student learns the Name of the Wind, they will have the same percentage chance of learning new names regardless of whether they are currently attending the University (including if they are expelled).

    • Warning: As a Namer learns new Names, their sanity is affected. For each Name beyond the first that they learn, their insanity roll bonus increases by one per Name. Additionally, each time they use more than 1 Name in a single turn, their insanity bonus increases.
    • First level: Namer learns the Name of the Wind. Each Name learned after the first gains a (+1) bonus to the insanity roll.
    • Second level: Gain a Naming action period. Can use 2 Names at once. (+2) bonus to insanity rolls each time you use 2 Names in a turn (on top of the Names bonus).
    • Third level: Gain a second Naming action period. Can use 3 Names at once. (+4) bonus to insanity rolls each time you use 3 Names in a turn (on top of the Names bonus).
    • Master level: Gain a third Naming action period. Can use 4 Names at once. (+6) each time you use 4 Names in a turn (on top of the Names bonus). Note on the Master Namer: They know all 10 Names, but because they are the Master and have therefore proven themselves to be a competent and responsible Namer, their Naming knowledge insanity bonus is only (+3).
    Important Note: Names can only be invoked once per turn. Invoking a Name takes an action. This applies to every Name invoked, even if they’re tied to the same action.
    Multi-Name Order Example: Use Fire to kill all those who attack me this cycle and use Earth to prevent my death. (This action would take up two action periods and grant a +2 bonus to the insanity roll, along with at least +2 from knowing two Names (Fire and Earth).)
    Insanity Bonus Example: Araris is an El’the Namer who has been elevated by the Master Namer through E’lir and Re’lar. He knows 5 Names, so by default, he has a (+4) bonus to his insanity rolls. If he uses 3 of his Names in a turn, he will gain an additional (+4) bonus to his insanity roll. Therefore, his insanity roll will have a (+8) bonus, making it much more likely that he will crack and be sent to the Crockery.
    • Wind - Action Manipulation, Action Block, PM Spying, Attack
    • Iron - Artificery Items, Fae, Action Block, and Protection
    • Earth - Action Block, Action Protection, Attack Protection
    • Fire - Attack, Mass Attack, Item Destruction
    • Water - Action Block, Attack Protection, Action Manipulation
    • Blood - Action Manipulation, Attack Protection, Attack
    • Bone - Action Manipulation, Action Block, Attack Protection, Attack, Alchemy Items
    • Stone - Protection, Action Block, Items
    • Holly - Items, Fae Protection, Fae Action Block, Seeking Manipulation/Fae
    • Amber - Alchemy, Mommets
    Important Note: Be ready to state both the base action you’re going for and how each additional Name is affecting that action. You’ll want to place Naming action early so the Naming GM has a chance to work with you and decide the effect of the action.
