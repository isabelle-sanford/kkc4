Game Basics

Factions

  • Students

    The students at the University come from all across the Four Corners. They’re at the University to study, and nothing should interfere with a student and his (or her) pursuit of knowledge. Including Skindancers. With their regular studies interrupted, the students are doing all they can to rid the University of the Fae infiltrators.

    Win Condition: Expel or kill all the Skindancers.

  • Skindancers

    Fae creatures believed to have been exterminated by the Sithe centuries ago, the Skindancers are making one last stand at the University. A small group has infiltrated the students, taking over their bodies. They have one mission: eliminate the arcanists.

    Skindancers have access to a Google doc to plan and conspire. They start the game mixed with the students, and they are able to do anything a student can, including becoming a Master.

    Skindancers can sabotage 1 person per turn. Sabotages appear as if the person simply cracked, and they are sent to the Crockery. If the Skindancers sabotage someone already in the Crockery or an expelled student, that player dies, unless they are protected.

    Win Condition: Expel, kill, or drive insane all the students OR destroy as many fields as starting Skindancers, which must include Naming, Alchemy, and Archives.

Cycles

Cycles will be separated into 3 turns, each representing 1 month in the game. This means that each cycle will represent 1 full term at the University, since terms at the University are 3 months in length. Turns will be 36 hours long with 12-hour rollovers between each turn, making the cycles 144 hours of real time, and 108 hours of in-game time.

Turns will be mostly indistinguishable from each other. Players will be able to take the same number of actions each turn. The only difference between turns will be certain events—stipend payout, admissions, and lodging—that affect all players. Players have no control over stipend payout or admissions, but they have some minor control over lodging, in that they can choose where they want to stay.

Turn Event Order
  • Complaint-Filing (including complaint-relevant actions)
  • The Horns
  • All Non-Offensive Role and Item Actions, including Imre actions
  • All Offensive Actions
  • Insanity Roll
  • Elevations
  • Filing EP

Events Only Occurring Just Before the First Turn of Each Cycle

  • Stipend Payout
  • Admissions and Tuition
    • Masters halved
    • Inflations and Reductions
    • Vint increase
    • Decreased Tuition from Arithmetics
  • Lodging

Voting

Instead of lynching players, the daily vote will be used to file complaints with the Masters. Every player is able to cast 2 votes each turn. Each vote counts as a complaint lodged against that student, and every 2 complaints counts as 1 Discipline Point against the player, which could result in students being put On the Horns. Votes on insane players do not count (including for the purpose of tuition reduction).

Action Periods

All students have one (1) general action period (unless in Imre) which they can use for any ability they have access to, including the sabotage. Some fields provide additional action periods upon elevation. These action periods might be general or specific to the field, and might be provided on each elevation or only upon elevation to Master.

There are a maximum of four (4) action periods available. Depending on the number of elevations a player may have and in what arcanum field, some players may have only 1 or 2 action periods, while others may have 3 or 4 action periods.

While in Imre, you do not have access to the general action period or any field-specific action periods. Instead, you have access to all the Imre actions. There are five Imre locations and you are able to go to each location each time you are in Imre (until you try for your Talent Pipes, and the The Eolian is no longer available). This includes expelled students visiting Imre.

There are a number of passive actions that some players may have. These passive actions cannot be roleblocked. All active actions can be roleblocked and any action with a target can be redirected.

Writeup Reveals

All non-information gathering actions do not receive any information past whether you succeeded in attempting the action and whom you targeted. Unless an action specifically targets a player to learn something about them, you will not learn whether your action succeeded.

If someone is attacked and protected, the writeup will only state that “Someone was attacked, but survived!” and will not specify the particular player. (The player in question will be notified that they were attacked, but not by what.)

Writeups will announce elevations, but not in which field a particular student was elevated.This includes elevations to Master. The destruction of a field will also be announced, including which field was destroyed.

PMs

PMs are open and do not require an action. You can make one-on-one PMs or group PMs. To make PMs easier for us when it comes to counting PMs and also to reduce the chances of messing up when someone spies on someone else's PMs, please start a new PM every turn. Do not roll PMs over to the next turn. Thank you!

Application System

During sign-ups, players are encouraged to work on a submission to send to the GMs. There are three submission types and each submission type will affect your starting stats in different ways. You may only choose one submission type, so choose wisely. Please do not use AI-generated submissions.

  • Musical Talent

    All players will start with a base amount of musical talent, which each player will learn at the start of the game. However, you can increase this stat by recording yourself singing or playing an instrument (or both!) and submitting this recording to the GMs. Doing this will increase your odds of winning the Pipes at The Eolian.

  • Field Proficiency

    All players will start the game with 2 Elevation Points in a random field. However, you can increase the amount of EP you start the game with and control which field it goes in by writing an essay about a subject at the University that you wish to study. Be as creative as you like.

  • Inventory

    While players don’t usually start the game with an item, by submitting a piece of art you created, you will start the game with an item. Possible items to be gained by submitting art: nahlrout, ward, tenaculum, bloodless, gram, bone-tar. The last two items will only be possible for particularly impressive submissions.
